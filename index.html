The Problem

Music is personal.
Your tools aren't.

Spotify tells you what you listened to. Last.fm tracks your plays. But nowhere can you sit down, reflect on an album, and share that experience with people who actually care.

A great album deserves more than a stream count.

What exists

Passive trackers. Dense databases. Streaming algorithms. Tools built for consumption, not reflection.

What's missing

A social diary built around albums — with real opinions, real people, and a community that takes music seriously.

What is BELDISCO?

Letterboxd.
But for music.

BELDISCO is an album diary app. Log every album you listen to. Rate it, review it, mark it as a favorite. Build a living archive of your musical journey and share it with friends who are doing the same.

Built for music people. Built by music people.

Why BELDISCO?

Every alternative
misses the point.

Last.fm

The veteran. Solid recommendations, large user base. But it's passive — it records what you listened to, not what you thought. The app feels like 2009.

RateYourMusic

The enthusiast's choice. Deep cataloging, beloved by serious listeners. But it's a dense desktop website. A database, not a community.

Spotify

Brilliant at streaming. Social features are superficial and constantly axed. No reviews, no ratings, no diary. You consume. You don't reflect.

Album of the Year

Solid ratings aggregator. But no native app, no friend feed, no personal logging. Closer to Metacritic than Letterboxd.

Letterboxd

Not a music app — but the closest spiritual model. It proved a social diary built around a single medium can become a cultural institution. Nobody has done this for music. Yet.
